Image Origin Overview

Sapin-sapin


Abra province, Luzon

 Glutinous rice, coconut milk, and sugar are the ingredients of the vibrant, multi-layered Filipino delicacy known as sapin-sapin. Using natural substances like pandan, ube (purple yam), and indigenous fruits, each layer is frequently colored differently. It is renowned for its sweet flavor and chewy texture.

Laing


Bicol, Luzon

The Bicol region is the origin of the traditional Filipino cuisine Laing, which is renowned for its unique fusion of spicy, rich, and creamy flavors. Its main component is dried taro leaves (gabi), which are then boiled in thick coconut milk (gata) and flavored with ginger, garlic, and onions, among other aromatics.

Vigan Longganisa

Illocos Vigan, Luzon

Ilocos Sur's Vigan City is home to the Filipino sausage known as Vigan Longganisa. It is renowned for having a tangy, garlicky, and mildly spicy flavor. Typically small and plump, it is made with ground pork combined with garlic, soy sauce, vinegar, and spices. It is sometimes eaten with fried eggs and garlic fried rice.

Kinilaw


Southern Philippines

Like ceviche, kinilaw is a typical Filipino dish that is renowned for its bright, zesty, and fresh flavors. It is usually made with raw fish or seafood that has been marinated in vinegar, which, in a process called denaturation, "cooks" the fish.

Bicol Express


Bicol, Luzon

Made with pork, chiles, shrimp paste, and coconut milk, Bicol Express is a fiery Filipino cuisine from the Bicol area. It has a rich, creamy, and spicy flavor profile and is named after the Bicol Express railway train.
